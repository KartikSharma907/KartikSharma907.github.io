Publications

Research publications and academic contributions in computer vision and machine learning.

Research Areas

My current research focuses on several key areas in computer vision and machine learning:

  • AI Video Understanding: Working on transformer-based multi-label classification models with dynamic querying for action recognition in videos.
  • Object Detection & Segmentation: Implementing instance segmentation frameworks for detecting contraband items in cluttered environments.
  • Remote Sensing: Developing multivariate time series analysis methods for crop yield estimation using Landsat and Sentinel satellite data fusion.
  • Computer Vision Applications: Exploring real-world applications of deep learning in various domains.

Current Research Projects
AI Video Understanding at Samsung Research Institute
May 2021 - Present

Researching and developing scalable transformer-based models for multi-label action classification in videos. The project involves predicting actions being performed in images/videos (e.g., "A dog and a cat are playing with a ball"). Current focus is on incorporating dynamic querying to make the model more robust and scalable.

Technologies: Transformers, Multi-label Classification, Dynamic Querying, Video Processing
Crop Yield Estimation using Satellite Data Fusion
May 2021 - Present

Working as a research assistant at the Advanced Data Analytics and Parallel Technologies Lab, BITS Pilani. The project involves estimating crop yield using multivariate time series analysis of fusion of Landsat and Sentinel satellite data. This research has applications in precision agriculture and food security.

Technologies: Time Series Analysis, Satellite Data Fusion, Remote Sensing, Machine Learning
Instance Segmentation for Contraband Detection
March 2021 - Present

Research assistant at Central Electronics Engineering Research Institute (CSIR-CEERI), Pilani, working under Dr. Dhiraj Sangwan. The project focuses on developing an instance segmentation framework to recognize highly cluttered instances of contraband items in security applications.

Technologies: Instance Segmentation, Computer Vision, Security Applications, Deep Learning
